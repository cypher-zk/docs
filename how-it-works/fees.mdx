Cypher charges two types of fees depending on the market type. YesNo and MultiOutcome markets split a combined 2% fee between the protocol treasury and the market creator. Accuracy markets charge a single 20% platform fee drawn only from the loser pool — winners always get their full stake back plus a share of what losers contributed. Understanding how fees work helps you calculate expected returns before you bet, and helps market creators understand the economic incentive to run high-quality markets.

## Fee overview

| | **YesNo** | **MultiOutcome** | **Accuracy** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Protocol fee** | 0.5% of total pool | 0.5% of total pool | 20% of loser pool |
| **LP fee (creator)** | 1.5% of total pool | 1.5% of total pool | None |
| **Combined fee** | 2% of total pool | 2% of total pool | 20% of loser pool only |
| **Applied to** | Total pool at settlement | Total pool at settlement | Loser pool after settlement |
| **Fee base** | `total_staked` | `total_staked` | `loser_pool` (computed by MPC) |

## Protocol fee

The protocol fee (default **0.5%**) is transferred from each pool vault to the Cypher treasury wallet at the moment the Arcium settlement callback fires. The treasury address is stored in the global `CyperMarket` config account and can be updated by the platform admin.

The protocol fee sustains Cypher's infrastructure — indexer, backend, Arcium compute costs, and on-chain program maintenance.

## LP fee (creator incentive)

The LP fee (default **1.5%**) goes directly to the market **creator's** USDC wallet at settlement time, not to any liquidity pool. This is the creator's reward for posting a well-formed, accurate market.

<Tip>
  The LP fee is how Cypher aligns creator incentives with participant interests. A creator who posts a bad question, picks the wrong oracle, or disappears before resolving risks losing their $10 bond (see below) — while a creator who runs clean markets earns 1.5% of every pool that settles through their market. On a $1,000 total pool that's $15 USDC returned alongside the bond.
</Tip>

## Accuracy platform fee

Accuracy markets work differently. Because every participant gets their stake back, the "fee" can only come from the profit pool — which is the aggregate of what losers contributed above their returned stake. The protocol takes **20%** of the loser pool before distributing the remaining 80% to winners weighted by prediction accuracy.

**No LP fee exists for Accuracy markets.** The 20% platform fee goes entirely to the treasury.

## Example calculations

<Accordion title="YesNo / MultiOutcome example: $100 total pool">
  Suppose a YesNo market closes with a $100 USDC total pool across both sides.

  ```
  Total pool:     $100.00
  Protocol fee:     $0.50   (0.5% × $100)
  LP fee:           $1.50   (1.5% × $100)
  ─────────────────────────
  Prize pool:      $98.00   distributed to winners
  ```

  Winners receive their original stake back plus a proportional share of the losing side's contribution, both drawn from the $98 prize pool.

  **SDK calculation:**

  ```ts
  import { computeFees } from "@cypher-zk/sdk";

  const { protocolFee, lpFee, netAmount } = computeFees(100_000_000n, {
    protocolFeeRateBps: 50,   // 0.5%
    lpFeeRateBps:       150,  // 1.5%
  });

  // protocolFee: 500_000   → $0.50 USDC
  // lpFee:       1_500_000 → $1.50 USDC
  // netAmount:  98_000_000 → $98.00 USDC (prize pool)
  ```
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Accuracy example: 4 participants, $10 tier">
  Suppose 4 participants enter a $10 Accuracy market. Total staked = $40.

  After MPC settlement, 2 participants are ranked "close" (winners) and 2 are ranked "far" (losers). All 4 get their $10 stake returned. The loser pool is:

  ```
  Loser pool:   2 × $10 = $20.00  (losers' stakes redistributed as profit)
  Platform fee:             $4.00  (20% × $20)
  ─────────────────────────────────
  Winner prize pool:        $16.00 distributed by proximity weight
  ```

  Each winner still receives their full $10 stake back. On top of that, the $16 prize pool is split by the accuracy weighting formula — the closer predictor takes the larger share.

  **Note:** The `computeFees` function is not used for Accuracy markets in the same way. Accuracy fees are computed by the Cypher backend after the MPC circuit reveals the loser count and sent to the treasury automatically as part of the settlement process.
</Accordion>

## Creator bond

To create any market — YesNo, MultiOutcome, or Accuracy — the creator must post a **$10 USDC bond** (`CREATOR_BOND = 10_000_000` raw units). This bond is held in a separate `Bond` vault account and is never mixed with pool funds.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Bond returned" icon="circle-check">
    After a successful, undisputed resolution the bond is transferred back to the creator's USDC wallet automatically when they call `withdrawCreatorFunds`.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Bond slashed — missed deadline" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    If the creator fails to post a resolution before `resolve_deadline`, the bond is slashed to the treasury and all participants can claim refunds.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Bond slashed — dispute upheld" icon="xmark">
    If someone flags the posted resolution and the admin confirms it was wrong, the bond is slashed to the treasury. The admin posts a corrected outcome.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  The creator bond applies equally to all market types. There is no higher bond for more complex markets — the minimum is always $10 USDC. The bond cannot be withdrawn until all pools in the market group have fully settled.
</Note>

## `computeFees` utility

Use `computeFees` to preview exactly how much will be deducted from a bet amount before submitting a transaction. The function mirrors the on-chain arithmetic and accepts raw USDC amounts (bigint with 6 decimal places).

```ts
import { computeFees, isBetAmountValid } from "@cypher-zk/sdk";
import type { FeeRates } from "@cypher-zk/sdk";

const rates: FeeRates = {
  protocolFeeRateBps: 50,   // from GlobalStateAccount.protocolFeeRate
  lpFeeRateBps:       150,  // from GlobalStateAccount.lpFeeRate
};

// Check validity before computing
const valid = isBetAmountValid(5_000_000n, rates);
// true — $5 USDC clears the minimum and produces non-zero net amount

const breakdown = computeFees(5_000_000n, rates);
// {
//   amount:      5_000_000n  → $5.00  (gross input)
//   protocolFee:    25_000n  → $0.025
//   lpFee:          75_000n  → $0.075
//   netAmount:   4_900_000n  → $4.90  (counted toward your pool position)
// }
```

Always read `protocolFeeRateBps` and `lpFeeRateBps` live from `GlobalStateAccount` rather than hardcoding the defaults — they can be updated by the platform admin.
