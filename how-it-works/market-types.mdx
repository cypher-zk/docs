title: "Prediction Market Types: YesNo, Multi, and Accuracy"
sidebarTitle: "Market Types"
description: "Cypher supports three prediction market types: binary YesNo, MultiOutcome with up to 4 options, and Accuracy markets for exact-value predictions."
---

Choosing the right market type shapes everything about how participants bet, how payouts are calculated, and how the oracle resolves the outcome. Cypher offers three distinct market structures — each designed for a different class of prediction. Binary questions ("Will X happen?") fit YesNo markets. Questions with a small set of named outcomes fit MultiOutcome. Questions that resolve to a specific number ("What will the BTC price be?") fit Accuracy markets, where closeness to the answer matters more than picking a winner outright.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="YesNo">
    ## YesNo markets

    A YesNo market poses a binary question — the outcome is either **YES** or **NO**. Participants pick one side and stake any amount of USDC above the minimum. The total pool flows to the winning side after fees.

    ### How outcomes work

    The oracle (Pyth price feed or the market creator) posts a `ResolvedValue::YesNo(bool)` — `true` for YES, `false` for NO. After the challenge window closes, the Arcium MPC circuit evaluates all encrypted positions, determines which side each participant chose, and writes individual payouts.

    **Payout formula:**

    ```
    payout = your_net_stake + (your_net_stake / winner_pool_net) × loser_pool_net
    ```

    Your net stake is your gross stake minus the protocol and LP fees deducted at settlement.

    ### Stake model

    - **Variable stakes** — you choose how much to stake, from the minimum up to any amount
    - **Minimum stake:** $0.10 USDC (`MIN_STAKE = 100_000` in raw units with 6 decimals)
    - Fees are applied to the total pool at settlement, not at bet time

    ### Pool capacity

    Each side (YES and NO) is backed by a separate pool. Each pool holds a **maximum of 8 positions**. Once a pool reaches capacity, no further bets are accepted for that side. This limit is enforced by the on-chain program and mirrors the fixed array size in the Arcium settlement circuit.

    ### Oracle support

    | Oracle | How it works |
    |---|---|
    | **Pyth** | Trustless price feed; outcome posted automatically by the feed |
    | **CreatorResolved** | Market creator posts the result; requires a $10 USDC bond |

    <Note>
      Pool capacity is 8 positions **per side** — so a YesNo market can accommodate up to 16 total positions (8 YES + 8 NO). Plan your market accordingly if you expect high participation.
    </Note>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="MultiOutcome">
    ## MultiOutcome markets

    A MultiOutcome market offers between **2 and 4 named outcomes**. Participants pick exactly one outcome and stake USDC. All stakes on losing outcomes flow to the winning outcome's pool (minus fees).

    ### How outcomes work

    Outcome labels are embedded directly in the on-chain question string using a `[Label A|Label B|Label C]` suffix. The SDK parses this suffix to display clean question text and bet buttons separately.

    ```ts
    import { parseEmbeddedOptions, getMarketOptionLabels } from "@cypher-zk/sdk";

    // Raw question stored on-chain:
    // "Who wins the 2025 F1 championship? [Verstappen|Hamilton|Norris]"

    const { displayQuestion, optionLabels } = parseEmbeddedOptions(rawQuestion);
    // displayQuestion: "Who wins the 2025 F1 championship?"
    // optionLabels:    ["Verstappen", "Hamilton", "Norris"]

    // Or use getMarketOptionLabels for canonical labels (handles YesNo fallback too):
    const labels = getMarketOptionLabels(market, rawQuestion);
    // ["Verstappen", "Hamilton", "Norris"]
    ```

    <Warning>
      Always pass the **raw question** (with the `[…]` suffix intact) to `getMarketOptionLabels`. Pass only `displayQuestion` to your UI rendering layer. Stripping the suffix before passing to `getMarketOptionLabels` will fall back to generic "Outcome 1, Outcome 2, …" labels.
    </Warning>

    The oracle posts a `ResolvedValue::Outcome(u8)` — the zero-based index of the winning outcome. The MPC circuit then evaluates all encrypted positions and routes the loser pools to winning participants.

    **Payout formula (same as YesNo, applied to the winning outcome pool):**

    ```
    payout = your_net_stake + (your_net_stake / winner_pool_net) × total_loser_pools_net
    ```

    ### Stake model

    - **Variable stakes** — same as YesNo
    - **Minimum stake:** $0.10 USDC
    - No fee difference from YesNo: protocol fee (0.5%) + LP fee (1.5%) on the total pool

    ### Pool capacity

    Each outcome has its own pool with a **maximum of 8 positions**. A market with 3 outcomes can hold up to 24 total positions (8 per outcome).

    ### Oracle support

    Both **Pyth** and **CreatorResolved** are supported, identical to YesNo markets.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Accuracy">
    ## Accuracy markets

    Accuracy markets ask participants to predict an **exact numeric value** — for example, the BTC/USD price at a specific time, or a stock's closing price. Instead of picking a winner, participants are ranked by how close their prediction is to the actual answer. Closer predictions earn a larger share of the loser pool.

    ### How outcomes work

    The oracle posts a `ResolvedValue::Numeric(u64)` — the real-world answer scaled by 1000 (e.g. $152.50 → `152_500`). The Arcium MPC circuit computes the absolute error for each encrypted prediction and assigns payout weights using a proximity curve:

    ```
    weight = (1 / (1 + error / median_error)) ^ 6
    ```

    Participants with errors below the median earn a larger share; those above earn less. Every participant receives their original stake back plus a weighted share of the loser pool (after the platform fee).

    ### Stake model

    Accuracy markets use **fixed stake tiers** — you choose which tier to enter, and the stake is exactly that amount:

    | Tier | Stake | SDK Constant |
    |---|---|---|
    | Micro | **$1 USDC** | `MICRO_BET_SIZE = 1_000_000` |
    | Standard | **$10 USDC** | `STANDARD_BET_SIZE = 10_000_000` |
    | Whale | **$100 USDC** | `WHALE_BET_SIZE = 100_000_000` |

    Each tier is a separate on-chain market with its own pool. Entering a $1 tier and a $10 tier for the same question counts as two independent positions.

    ### Pool capacity

    Each tier pool holds a **maximum of 4 positions**. The Accuracy settlement circuit uses a fixed 4-slot array. An Accuracy market group therefore supports up to 12 total positions across all three tiers.

    ### Fee model

    Accuracy markets have **no LP fee**. Instead, a **20% platform fee** is taken from the loser pool only — not the total staked. Winners always receive their full stake back plus their share of the net loser pool.

    ### Oracle support

    Both **Pyth** and **CreatorResolved** are supported.

    <Tip>
      Accuracy markets reward research and precision rather than directional bias. If you have a well-founded quantitative estimate, an Accuracy market often offers better expected value than a binary market on the same underlying question.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>

## Market type comparison

| | **YesNo** | **MultiOutcome** | **Accuracy** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Outcomes** | 2 (YES / NO) | 2–4 named options | Numeric value |
| **Minimum stake** | $0.10 USDC | $0.10 USDC | $1 / $10 / $100 USDC (fixed tiers) |
| **Stake model** | Variable | Variable | Fixed per tier |
| **Pool capacity** | 8 per side | 8 per outcome | 4 per tier |
| **Protocol fee** | 0.5% of total pool | 0.5% of total pool | 20% of loser pool |
| **LP fee** | 1.5% of total pool | 1.5% of total pool | None |
| **Pyth oracle** | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ |
| **Creator-resolved** | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ |
| **Winner determined by** | Correct side | Correct outcome | Proximity to answer |
